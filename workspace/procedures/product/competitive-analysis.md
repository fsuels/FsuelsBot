## What to Track

### Weekly Check (15 min)

| Element | What to Note |
|---------|--------------|
| Homepage | Hero offer, featured products |
| New Arrivals | What they're adding |
| Sale section | What's discounted (dead stock?) |
| Bestsellers | What's working for them |
| Pricing | Any changes? |

### Monthly Deep Dive (1 hour)

| Element | Analysis |
|---------|----------|
| Product range | Categories, depth |
| Pricing strategy | Price points, discounts |
| Marketing | Ads, email, social |
| Reviews | What customers love/hate |
| UX | Site experience, checkout |
| Content | Blog, guides, social |

---

## How to Analyze

### 1. Subscribe to Everything
- Email list (use separate email)
- SMS list
- Follow on social
- Set Google Alerts

### 2. Shop Their Store
- Add to cart (trigger abandonment emails)
- Buy something (see post-purchase flow)
- Return something (see process)

### 3. Use Spy Tools
- **SimilarWeb** - Traffic estimates
- **Facebook Ad Library** - See their ads
- **SEMrush/Ahrefs** - Keywords they rank for
- **BuiltWith** - Tech stack
- **Social Blade** - Social growth

### 4. Read Their Reviews
Best market research = what customers say:
- What do they love?
- What do they complain about?
- What do they wish existed?

## Competitive Gaps to Find

| Gap Type | Opportunity |
|----------|-------------|
| Missing products | Offer what they don't |
| Poor reviews | Do that thing better |
| Slow shipping | Be faster |
| Bad service | Be friendlier |
| High prices | Be more affordable |
| Boring content | Be more engaging |
| Weak social | Out-content them |

## What NOT to Do

❌ Copy everything blindly
❌ Obsess over competitors
❌ Race to lowest price
❌ Assume they know better

**They have problems too.** Focus 80% on YOUR customers, 20% on competitors.
