# Google Merchant Center (GMC) Reinstatement — Best Practices (Merchant Center + Shopify)

## Scope

Practical checklist to fix policy issues and request re-review when using the **Google & YouTube app on Shopify**.

## Key principles (what Google is looking for)

- **Be upfront, honest, and consistent** across your website + Merchant Center data.
- Google treats **Misrepresentation** as an _egregious_ violation; appeals only succeed when changes are **thorough and truthful**.

## High-probability fixes (website + account)

### 1) Contact information (must be easy to find)

- At least one clear contact method on-site (contact form, email, phone, social profile).

### 2) Return / refund policy

- Must exist and clearly state timelines/requirements.
- If you don’t accept returns, explicitly say so.

### 3) Shipping information

- Clear shipping terms and info before purchase.
- Avoid missing or hidden shipping conditions.

### 4) Consistency of offers

- Don’t advertise products you can’t deliver or aren’t available.
- Availability/price must match what Google crawls.

### 5) Identity and trust

- Don’t imply affiliation/certification you don’t have.
- Avoid any misleading business identity or contact details.

## How to request re-review (Shopify path)

From Google & YouTube app in Shopify:

1. Shopify admin → **Sales channels** → **Google**
2. **Settings** tab
3. Under “Your Google Merchant Center account” → **Request re-review**
4. Complete the form (be specific about what you fixed)

> Google notes review requests can take **up to 7 days**.

## Cooldown behavior

- If reviews keep failing, Google may impose a **cool down period** where the review button is disabled.
- Use cooldown to fix root causes; repeated re-requests without fixes makes it worse.
