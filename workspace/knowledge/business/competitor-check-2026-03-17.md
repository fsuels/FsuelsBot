# Competitor Check — 2026-03-17

_Context: Dress Like Mommy — mommy-and-me outfits market_
_Frequency: Should run monthly minimum, weekly during seasonal peaks_

## PatPat

- **Promo observed:** Homepage messaging references "Easter Outfit Sale 2026" + "Flash Sale Up to 50% Off" (source: Brave snippet; not fully price-verified).
- **Collection positioning:** Broad SKU range (dresses, tees, pajamas, swimsuits, rompers) with seasonal/holiday framing (Easter, Mother's Day).
- **Pricing strategy:** Aggressive discounting as primary value prop. "Compare at" prices suggest MSRP anchoring.
- **Source:** https://www.patpat.com/collections/mommy-and-me-outfits
- **Lesson for DLM:** PatPat competes on price volume. DLM cannot win on price — must compete on brand, quality perception, and niche positioning.

## SHEIN / Amazon / Etsy

- **NO_CITABLE_EVIDENCE:** Brave Search rate-limited in this run, so pricing/promos were not verified.
- **Known from prior research:** SHEIN dominates ultra-low-price; Amazon has broad selection but weak brand story; Etsy has handmade/boutique positioning.
- **Action for next check:** Use direct URL navigation (Chrome automation) to capture current pricing and promos for these three competitors.

## Opportunities (Ranked by Impact)

1. **Seasonal landing pages + internal links** (Easter / Mother's Day) to match competitor intent capture. Competitors are already doing this — DLM is not.
2. **Add clear promo framing** (time-boxed) on relevant collections, without competing purely on price. "Limited Spring Collection" beats "50% Off" for brand positioning.
3. **Content differentiation:** PatPat has zero lifestyle content. DLM could win on blog/social storytelling around the mommy-and-me experience.

## Threats

- PatPat continues heavy discount framing; competing on price alone is not sustainable for DLM.
- Amazon and SHEIN have unlimited catalog depth — DLM must be narrower and more curated.
- If GMC remains blocked, paid advertising cannot counter competitor visibility.
